About me

My name is Alex Lawrence. I am a Game Designer with 2 years Professional experience. I am Passionate about making experiences that anyone can enjoy, and I strive to always be improving. Moving forward, step by step.

I have experience making games as well as interactive experiences. I am proficient with the Unity and Unreal engines, and am experienced with C# and C++ code. I have designed web pages, including this one, using HTML and CSS. I also have experience with photoshop, modeling in Maya, and designing workflows in Figma.

Whenever I am not programming or gaming, I enjoy writing stories and thinking up new characters. I particularly enjoy framing potential character interaction or action scenes. You can always find a few scenarios banging around in my head.

Projects

Doots Never Die
Doots Never Die is a game made in Unity in fall 2021 by me and a team of RIT students. The game is a top-down shooter where you're fighting against the clock to scare as many kids as possible on Halloween night. The game was fully programmed in Unity and published to the web using the built-in webGL functionality. My role in Doots Never Die was on the enemy AI of the children in the game. I had to figure out how the enemies would react to the player's position at all times and carefully balance their speed and aggressiveness to make the game challenging, but fair. WebGL build of the game

Wilderwood
Wilderwood is a 3D collectathon made in Unity by me and a group of friends. As lead UI Designer/Developer, I was in charge of prototyping and refining the look and feel of the our interface. The design is simple and rustic looking, which I thought best fit the elements of our casual experience, but after multiple iterations, it was also streamlined to give the player the information they needed without being too crowded and messy.

Submerged
Submerged is a game made in fall 2021 in Unity by me and a team of RIT students The game is a side-scrolling platformer that takes place entirely underwater. As a result, the movement is floaty and the character relies on a grappling hook for quick lateral movement and manuverability. The game was made in Unity using the WWIse sound engine. My role on Submerged was head level designer. I made levels that introduced and reinforced a core mechanic of the gameplay and was also key in asset creation for the look and feel of the game, like the backgrounds for the levels and menu.
